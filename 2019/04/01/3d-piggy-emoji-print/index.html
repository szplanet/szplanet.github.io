Piggy Emoji Mold

Step 1: Prepare CAD Model

I used SOLIDWORKS to create a piggy sketch. Rather than a simple 3D printed part, I added a cylinder shaped pouring cup to make sure that I can pour liquids in the mold later. Additionally, I added a small riser for air to escape after liquids are pour in. To make a clean cast, I introduced some draft to the part as well to reduce sharp edges so that the parts can come off easily.

Step 2: 3D Print

I used a laser drawn resin 3D printer because it creates a smoother finish. The part is placed this way to reduce the amount of support system.

Step 3: Create Silicone Mold

I used a laser drawn resin 3D printer because it creates a smoother finish. The part is placed this way to reduce the amount of support system. Liquid silicone is prepared by mixing a 2-part solution. A vacuum chamber is used to remove any air bubbles within the liquid. After pouring the liquid into the cylinder and leaving the tips of the pouring cup and riser outside, I placed it onto a vibrating table to further remove air bubbles so that the model is dense and void free. Finally, I sliced open the mold on the long edge after the solution is cured. Voila, the silicone mold is in the shape of a piggy emoji!
