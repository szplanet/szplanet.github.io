Becoming a Non-Passive Reader

My book tracking system to beat the forgetting curve.

Posted by Sherry Zhang on Tuesday, February 9, 2021

Table of Content

  • Problem
  • Approach and Next Steps

Problem

You read a great book, highlighted your favorite quotes, but a few months later, you forgot most of the things in the book and never revisit the notes you took. Does that happen to you? Well, it happens to me all the time. I can vaguely remember the gist and my impressions of the books I’ve read, and overtime, I return everything back to my “teachers”.

I used read a lot of random books, especially detective novels, as a kid until school/textbook reading occupied most of my time. Since then, reading somehow felt forced. I no longer sought out time to read for leisure. Transitioning from college to the working world, I have a new found appreciation for reading. I find myself reading more non-fiction and biography books as a way to make sense of the “crazy” world around me and seek guidance from the “lessons learned” by many others. Digging into a book during my weekly air commutes and hotel stays lets me to take refuge from work. During quarantine, the perfect Sunday afternoon is when I lounge in my comfy couch, with a cup of green tea at hand, and read as I get ready for the week. I resort to reading as a way to discover things that I wouldn’t easily come across in my day-to-days and fill myself in with topics such as psychology, history, and economics, which I didn’t get to study as much in school.

However, unlike computers, we are forgetful. Writing this blog helps me remember things, so I want to find a solution to help me better remember books.

Approach and Next Steps

I recently came across a video, How I Remember Everything I Read, from Ali Abdaal on YouTube. He talks about the different (7) levels of reading and setting up a system to briefly summarize each book we’ve read so that we can revisit our own spark notes version of these books at any time. I got inspired from it and decided to borrow his template to start. I hope writing down my own interpretations of each book will not only help me to become a better thinker and writer but also remind me to intentionally apply those learnings.

His template separates books into Fiction and Non-Fiction. Depending on the genre, different sets of prompts will show up, such as how I discovered it, how the book changed me, and summarizing the book in a few sentences. I started jotting down my notes for each book that I recently read in this template. As I go along, I will customize it based on things that I hope to capture. I wish I started doing this earlier haha! I also have a long list of books that I want to read, so I’ve created a “to-read” book list with rankings for each book to help me prioritize.
