On Painting

Another way to express myself.

Posted by Sherry Zhang on Thursday, January 28, 2021

Table of Content

Can’t believe it’s already 2021 and we’ve been in pandemic mode for almost a year now. It had been a challenging year for many and a strange year for me as well, with many ups and downs. I had to adjust my life around it for quite a bit, but I’m glad to have family around and friends a phone call away (plus the weather in OC is superb).

“My 2020 was an X-Ray that revealed everything that I needed to work on and it showed me everything that was important to me." – From Jenn Im in How 2020 Changed Me

Since traveling is still off the limit, I found a new “indoor” hobby - PAINTING! I remember having art requirements back in high school, but I’ve only resorted to band and photography classes because I didn’t think I could draw or paint. I do enjoy the arts and I definitely came to appreciate it a lot more through out the years so I figured, what’s a better time to branch out and give it a shot than now?

My Gallery: this is a growing Google Photos “virtual” gallery. I keep digital versions of my paintings here and include some back stories for each one.

To start, I purchased some paint, brushes, and canvas paper from Michael’s (I also became their rewards member from this trip) and followed a bunch of YouTubers and their tutorials titled “beginners painting.” After laying some newspaper on the coffee table, I got right into the works. I put in my own twists and interpretations of those paintings, or it could also be the fact that I can’t paint as good as my virtual teachers (yet lol).

  • Calmness: The process of mixing, painting, and even cleaning is actually so soothing and calming. It helps me to clear my mind because I only need to focus on the canvas right in front of me and think about how to get certain colors, which brush to use, or where to place my strokes.

  • Imagination and Self-Expression: I enjoy the creative process and letting my mind travel beyond the piece itself. When painting the beach scenes, I imagine myself listening to the sound of the waves and feeling the breeze on my face as if I was right there. When drawing people and figures, I think about what kind of personalities they would have as if I was creating characters with feelings and emotions. This also lets me to express my thoughts and emotions.

  • Techniques: As someone who only did “engineering sketches” on 3D design softwares, I am fascinated by the process of adding on layers in different shades or colors to create more depth in the painting. This requires such keen observations and conceptualizations to make 2D look 3D. I have a lot more work ahead.

From knowing nothing about color theories to doing “wet-on-wet” blending, I’m learning a lot from practicing and web researching. I want to get better at it. I’d like to paint something of my own creations as I progress and make room for a “creative corner” inside my home as I realized that this is something that’s very important to me.
